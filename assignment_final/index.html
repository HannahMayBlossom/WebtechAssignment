English Language and Literature

My undergraduate degree was in English Language and Literature where my main focus was on Lingustics.
My dissertation was on Young Adult novels and whether the female main leads where 'good' role models for female readers.
Primarily, my focus was on feminism; how langauge was used and how it could be altered to greater effect. Due to this, I was always intriguied how lagnuage can affect peoples views and how it was used to persuade others.
I researched into lots of different areas of Lingustics. One area was children's fiction, and even got the oppotunity to go to a seminar on Children's Fiction.
I also had the amazing chance to do something different and research Science-Fiction and Fantasy novels, an area in lingustics and even literature that isn't commonly researched. I enjoyed researching into tokenism and stylistics.
Naturally, as a literature student I read and anaylsed several different era's of novels, from Shakespeare, to Jane Austen and post-modernism. While I enjoyed reading and anylsisng lots of material, I prefered the area of linguistics as allowed for a more interesting text-analysis.
